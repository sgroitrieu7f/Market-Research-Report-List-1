<p><p>The sugar substitutes market consists of three main types: high-intensity sweeteners, low-intensity sweeteners, and high fructose syrup. High-intensity sweeteners, such as aspartame and sucralose, deliver a sweetness much greater than sugar, often with little to no calories. Low-intensity sweeteners, like sucrose and glucose, provide a milder sweetness similar to sugar but with varying caloric content. High fructose syrup, derived from corn, offers a sweet taste and is commonly used in processed foods for its cost-effectiveness and sweetness profile.</p></p>
