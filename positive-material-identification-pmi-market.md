<p><p>Positive Material Identification (PMI) is a non-destructive testing technique used to determine the composition of materials, ensuring they meet specifications for safety and quality. In the PMI market, two primary technologies prevail: X-Ray Fluorescence (XRF), which analyzes elemental composition by detecting emitted X-rays from materials, and Optical Emission Spectrometry (OES), which utilizes light emitted from excited atoms to identify elements. Both methods play crucial roles in various industries, including manufacturing, construction, and metallurgy, to prevent material failures.</p></p>
