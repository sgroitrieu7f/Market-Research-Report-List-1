<p><p>The posterior spinal system screws market primarily features two types: uniaxial screws and polyaxial screws. Uniaxial screws are fixed in one direction, providing stability for specific spinal alignments, making them suitable for certain surgical applications. In contrast, polyaxial screws offer a greater range of motion, allowing for multi-directional placement to adapt to varying anatomy and improve surgical flexibility. Both types are critical for spinal stabilization and are widely used in orthopedic and neurosurgical procedures.</p></p>
